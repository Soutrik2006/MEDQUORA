Sexual Health Guidelines

Knowledge, care, and protection — for a healthy sexual life.

❤️ What is Sexual Health?

Sexual health is a state of physical, emotional, mental, and social well-being related to sexuality. It involves safe practices, respect, consent, and access to health services, not just the absence of disease or dysfunction.

🛡️ Safe Practices

  • 🧼 Maintain proper hygiene before and after sexual activity
  • 🧤 Use condoms to protect against STIs and unplanned pregnancy
  • 🩺 Get regular screenings for STIs (HIV, chlamydia, gonorrhea, etc.)
  • 💬 Communicate openly with your partner about boundaries
  • 🙅‍♀️ Avoid risky behavior like multiple unprotected partners

Safe sex is responsible sex.

💊 Common Medications & Treatments

  • Antibiotics: For bacterial infections like chlamydia or gonorrhea
  • Antivirals: For managing herpes and HIV (e.g., acyclovir, ART)
  • Emergency Contraception: Used after unprotected intercourse
  • Hormonal Therapy: For reproductive health and regulation
  • HPV Vaccine: Protects against cervical cancer and genital warts

💡 General Tips

  • 🧠 Educate yourself and your partner about consent and rights
  • 🏥 Visit a gynecologist or urologist for any unusual symptoms
  • 🧴 Use appropriate lubricants to reduce discomfort
  • 💑 Build trust and emotional connection with your partner
  • 📞 Reach out to support centers for confidential help when needed
